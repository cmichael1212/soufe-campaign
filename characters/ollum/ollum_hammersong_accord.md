# The Hammersong Accord

*A meeting between King Ollum Hammersong of Hammersong Hall and Lord Dagult Neverember, Open Lord of Waterdeep and Lord Protector of Neverwinter. The Palace of Neverwinter, late in the timeskip years.*

---

## The Chamber

*Ollum is shown to a side chamber in the Palace of Neverwinter. Not the throne room — Neverember is not granting a formal audience. A long table, maps of the Sword Coast spread across it, a fire in the hearth. Wine on the table, not ale. Two guards by the door, more decorative than threatening.*

*Lord Dagult Neverember enters without ceremony. He is older than Ollum remembers — heavier in the jaw, silver at the temples — but the eyes are the same. Sharp. Calculating. The eyes of a man who weighs every word on a merchant's scale.*

> *"King Hammersong."* He says the title without mockery, but also without deference. Testing it in his mouth. *"Sit. I understand you have a proposal for me."*
>
> *"Tell me about this forge of yours. What can Hammersong Hall offer that I can't get from Mirabar for half the trouble?"*

---

## Ollum's Pitch

Ollum rises to his full hill-dwarf height, gauntleted hand on his chest.

> "Greetings, Lord Protector! Look upon my glorious visage and rejoice, for I am Ollum Hammersong — rightful King of Hammersong Hall, defender of the High Forest, and the sharpest-tongued hero this side of the Lost Peaks! I stand before you not just with a shield of devotion, but with a song of mutual prosperity. Let me tell you how we're gonna make both our kingdoms filthier rich than a dragon nappin' on a pile of gold!"
>
> "The High Forest holds riches that Neverwinter needs, and Neverwinter holds the ports my people require. Now listen here, Lord Protector — before you reply, Hammersong Hall is sittin' on a goldmine. Well, technically a *mithral* mine. And timber so thick it'd make a giant's axe weep! I'm talkin' top-shelf, high-grade High Forest lumber and metal so pure it sings, and I'm willin' to ship it straight to your docks!"
>
> "In return, I want your finest craftsmen, woodcutters, and wagon-loads of supplies to turn my hall into the grandest fortress the bards have ever sung about! Let us draft a treaty that fills both our treasuries and secures our borders."

---

## Neverember Considers

*Neverember pours the wine himself. Two glasses. Slides one across without asking.*

*For just a heartbeat — half a heartbeat — something passes across his face. Not quite surprise. Not quite respect. The look of a man who came to this table expecting to weigh a sack of grain and finds himself reaching for an ingot instead. It is gone before Ollum can be sure he saw it.*

> *"King Hammersong."* The title comes easier this time. No more testing the shape of it. *"Sit. Drink. You have my attention — which is more than most who have stood where you stand can say."*

*He swirls his glass.*

> *"Three things, and we will know whether there is a treaty between us. The mithral — by weight, not by song. The road between your hall and my docks — who keeps it, and what happens when it breaks. And the question of whether Neverwinter is your partner, your first customer, or merely a stop on a longer road."*

*He looks at Ollum over the rim of the glass.*

> *"And one thing more. My roads are not what they were. Neither are my streets. Neverwinter has troubles I did not have a season ago — troubles I am not yet ready to name aloud. Any treaty we sign, your wagons will be moving through them. If you have something to offer on that, speak now. If not, that is your choice. Either way, I will remember."*

*He sets the glass down. Waits.*

---

## Ollum's Reply

Ollum slams his gauntleted hand on the table, leaning in with a wild grin.

> "Why, bless my brass buttons, Lord Protector, now you're talkin' like a dwarf with a plan! Keep that wine flowin' and listen close!"
>
> "First off, that mithral is as pure as a maiden's sigh and heavier than a disappointed mother-in-law! You'll get it weighed fair and square on standard scales — no bardic metrics required, though a good tune does make the hauling lighter!"
>
> "As for that road? Hammersong Hall keeps our end clean, and we expect your boys to hold yours. If the line breaks, my party and I will bounce on 'em like a pack of starving wolverines! You ain't just a stop on the map, Neverember — you're our exclusive premiere partner on the Sword Coast! Nobody else gets a sniff of this metal until Neverwinter's coffers are bursting!"

He lowers his voice to a booming, conspiratorial stage-whisper, tapping the holy symbol on his chest.

> "Now, about these secret troubles in your streets... You're lookin' at a Paladin of the righteous Haela Brightaxe, a hero of olde song! If you got shadows creepin' in your back alleys or bad hombres makin' a fuss, my hammer is itchy, my voice is primed, and my boys can clean up a mess faster than a grease fire in a bacon factory! You name the trouble, and consider it well and truly walloped!"

Ollum reaches into his pack with a flourish, pulling out a massive, slightly crumpled scroll tied with a bright red ribbon. He thumps it down onto the table right next to Neverember's wine glass.

> "Well, butter my biscuits, Lord Protector! It's your lucky day, 'cause I just so happen to have the official, legal-eagle, iron-clad treaty right here! Plucked it straight out of my head and onto the parchment before we set sail!"

---

## The Accord

The treaty Ollum produces is hand-lettered, dramatically titled, and tied with a red ribbon. It reads:

> 📜 **THE GRAND TRIUMPHANT ACCORD OF MUTUAL PROSPERITY**
>
> *Drafted by the Hand of His Royal Loudness, King Ollum Hammersong*
>
> **ARTICLE I: THE GOOD STUFF (THE SUPPLY)**
> Hammersong Hall hereby agrees to ship loads of top-grade iron ore, High Forest lumber, and authentic, shiny Mithral down the road. Neverwinter gets the first look at every wagon. If it ain't heavy enough to break a mule's back, Ollum will personally apologize in song.
>
> **ARTICLE II: THE PAYBACK (THE RETURN)**
> In exchange for the aforementioned shiny metals, the City of Neverwinter will immediately dispatch a small army of their finest stone-masons, architects, and carpenters to Hammersong Hall. Furthermore, Neverwinter will provide barrel-loads of flour, salt pork, and fine elven wine (for the King's personal creative inspiration).
>
> **ARTICLE III: ROAD PROTECTION AND MONSTER WALLOPING**
> The party known as "Hammersong Hall's very own Hamer Singers" will maintain absolute security on the High Forest end of the trade route. They promise to keep the local orcs thoroughly thrashed and the resident Ancient Black Dragon properly behaved. Neverwinter guards will take over the escort duties once the wagons hit the main high road, ensuring no highwaymen get any funny ideas.
>
> **ARTICLE IV: THE EXCLUSIVITY CLAUSE**
> Neverwinter is hereby declared the Single, Only, and Most Important Best Friend of Hammersong Hall on the Sword Coast. No Waterdeep merchants, no Baldur's Gate swindlers, and no shady underdark folks get a single pebble of our Mithral until Neverwinter is satisfied.
>
> **ARTICLE V: THE "FIXER" ADDENDUM** *(Scrawled hastily in the margin in charcoal)*
> If Lord Neverember has spooky, secret troubles in his city streets, King Ollum and his crew will stomp, smash, and righteously wallop said troubles into oblivion at no extra charge, provided Neverwinter pays for the party's lodging and tavern tabs while they are in town.
>
> SIGNED UNDER PENALTY OF A THOROUGH BEARD-PULLING:
>
> X __
> *King Ollum Hammersong, The Hero of the Peaks*
>
> X __
> *Lord Dagult Neverember, Lord Protector (Sign Here)*

---

## Neverember Reads

*Neverember picks the scroll up. Unties the red ribbon with the patience of a man who has untied many ribbons in his life. Unrolls it on the table beside the maps. Reads, quietly, for the space of three breaths. His eyebrows do something small.*

*He sets it down. Looks at Ollum. And for the second time that evening, the flicker passes across his face. The same one as before. Half-surprise, half-something-else. He does not bother hiding it as quickly this time.*

> *"You wrote this yourself."*

*It is not quite a question.*

> *"On the boat. Before you knew whether I would even sit at the table with you. Before you knew whether I would laugh you out of the chamber or call the guards."*

*A pause. He picks up his wine. Drinks it properly this time. Sets the glass down.*

> *"King Hammersong. I came to this meeting expecting to talk a young dwarf down to a modest contract and send him home with a polite letter. Instead you have spent the last quarter of an hour selling me mithral I have not yet seen, a road I cannot yet trust, and — apparently — your own hammer thrown in at no extra cost. And what is worse, I find I am inclined to take all three."*

*He taps the scroll.*

> *"My factors will read this in the morning. They will quibble. That is what I pay them for. But you and I — yes. We have an understanding. Hammersong steel for Neverwinter silver. My craftsmen and carpenters will be on the High Road within the month — masons, joiners, a wright or two, lumber crews, and the supplies to keep them fed while they raise your hall. In return, the first shipments of mithral begin moving the season the road allows. I expect honest weight. I expect Hammersong axes around every wagon. And I expect the exclusivity you offered to mean what you said it means."*

*He extends his hand across the table. The merchant's handshake — firm, businesslike, no flourish.*

> *"As for the other matter."* The eyes harden, just slightly. *"You offered your hammer. I will not call on it tonight. But I will remember that you offered. And I think, King Hammersong, that before our treaty is six months old, you and I will be speaking of it again. Sooner, perhaps, than either of us would like."*

*He returns to the scroll. Reads it through, properly this time. His face does not move — except, once, halfway down, when he reaches Article III and his eyes pause for exactly one heartbeat.*

> *"...an Ancient Black Dragon."*

*He looks up.*

> *"You did not mention an Ancient Black Dragon."*

*He returns to reading. His eyebrows perform a small, controlled exercise at the phrase "Hammersong Hall's very own Hamer Singers," but he says nothing. He reaches Article V — the charcoal scrawl in the margin — and a sound that might, in another man, have been a laugh escapes the very back of his throat. It is brief. It is gone.*

> *"Tavern tabs."*

*He sets the scroll down with the care due any document that may one day be entered as evidence in something.*

> *"King Hammersong. This will not be the version that survives. My factors will draft a fair copy by week's end — same terms, fewer mules, no beard-pulling clauses, and the dragon disclosed up front so I am not surprised by him later. You will sign that one."*

*A pause. He taps the original parchment with one finger.*

> *"But this — I am keeping. For my private collection."*

*He rises. Extends his hand a second time.*

> *"We have an accord, King Hammersong. Welcome to the Sword Coast."*

---

*The original Accord, in Ollum's hand, is kept among Lord Neverember's personal papers. The fair copy — duly signed, properly witnessed, with the Ancient Black Dragon disclosed up front — would be filed with the Neverwinter Merchant Guild three days later.*
